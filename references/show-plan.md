# show plan reference

Use this reference when ranking deals for first-show usefulness.

## Business context

- category: pokemon cards only
- sell channel: whatnot
- starting bankroll: $500
- first-show inventory target: 50 to 80 sellable items or lots
- first-show sell target: 20 to 35 items

## Desired inventory mix

### filler velocity items
Aim for 30 to 45 items.
Examples:
- low-cost singles
- holo and reverse-holo mini lots
- small themed lots
- japanese low-to-mid lots
- low-cost binder pulls

### mid-tier anchors
Aim for 15 to 25 items.
Examples:
- stronger modern hits
- recognizable vintage cards
- nicer japanese singles
- compact lots with clear upside

### headline items
Aim for 5 to 10 items.
Examples:
- the best singles in hand
- standout binder finds
- slabs or premium raw cards if available

## Ranking logic

Rate show usefulness as high when the deal:
- fills a weak bucket in the current show plan
- is easy to present on stream
- is likely to close without needing a huge audience
- can be broken into multiple sellable units

Rate show usefulness as medium when the deal:
- is profitable but requires more sorting, cleaning, or pricing work
- is a good buy but not ideal for the current show mix

Rate show usefulness as low when the deal:
- ties up too much bankroll
- is hard to explain live
- depends on grading or long holds
- is too niche for early Whatnot shows
